Road Safety Tips

General Road Safety Rules

  • Always wear a seatbelt, whether you are the driver or a passenger.
  • Adhere to speed limits and road signs at all times.
  • Do not use your mobile phone while driving. If necessary, use a hands-free system.
  • Never drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Keep a safe distance from the vehicle in front of you to allow for sudden stops.
  • Use indicators to signal your intentions to other road users.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and anticipate potential hazards.
  • Regularly check your vehicle’s condition, including brakes, tires, and lights.
  • Ensure children are seated in appropriate car seats and secured properly.
  • Respect pedestrian crossings and always give way to pedestrians.

Driver Safety Tips

  • Plan your route before you start driving to avoid distractions.
  • Take regular breaks on long journeys to avoid fatigue.
  • Adjust your driving according to weather conditions. Slow down in rain, fog, or snow.
  • Be extra cautious in residential areas and school zones.
  • Check blind spots before changing lanes or merging.
  • Use headlights appropriately, switching to low beam when following another vehicle or when another vehicle is approaching.
  • Keep both hands on the steering wheel and avoid eating or drinking while driving.
  • Be patient and avoid aggressive driving behaviors such as tailgating or road rage.

Pedestrian Safety Tips

  • Always use sidewalks where available. If not, walk facing oncoming traffic.
  • Cross streets at marked crosswalks or intersections whenever possible.
  • Look both ways before crossing the street, even if you have the right of way.
  • Stay visible at night by wearing reflective clothing or carrying a flashlight.
  • Avoid distractions such as using your phone while walking near traffic.
  • Follow pedestrian signals and do not cross when the signal is red.
  • Be cautious when walking near driveways or alleyways where vehicles may be entering or exiting.

Cyclist Safety Tips

  • Wear a helmet and other protective gear at all times.
  • Use bike lanes where available and follow the same rules as vehicles.
  • Make yourself visible with bright clothing and lights, especially at night or in low visibility conditions.
  • Signal your intentions to drivers and pedestrians using hand signals.
  • Stay alert and avoid listening to music or using your phone while cycling.
  • Regularly check your bike’s condition, including brakes, tires, and lights.
  • Be aware of parked cars and the possibility of doors opening unexpectedly.
